Sears Tower

A 110-story, 1,450-foot skyscraper, it's actual name is Willis Tower. At completion in 1973, it surpassed the World Trade Center in New York to become the tallest building in the world, a title it held for nearly 25 years; it remained the tallest building in the Western Hemisphere until the completion of a new building at the rebuilt World Trade Center site in 2014.
